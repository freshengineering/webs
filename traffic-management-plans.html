Traffic Management Plans & CTIA in Victoria

A Traffic Management Plan sets out how road users, pedestrians, cyclists and workers stay safe when works occupy a road reserve. In Victoria we design to the Austroads Guide to Temporary Traffic Management (AGTTM) and the DTP Victorian supplement, which has replaced the old VicRoads worksite rules.

A Construction Traffic Impact Assessment (CTIA) looks wider: how site traffic, truck routes and access affect the surrounding network over a project. Councils and DTP often make a CTIA a planning permit condition on bigger jobs. We prepare both across Melbourne metro and regional Victoria.

Frequently Asked Questions

When do I need a TMP in Victoria?

Whenever works occupy or affect a road, footpath or nature strip, including lane closures, parking removal or crossover construction. Council roads need a road occupation or consent-to-work permit; DTP-declared roads need a Memorandum of Authorisation.

What's the difference between a TMP and a CTIA?

A TMP is the on-site design to AGTTM, covering signs, devices and lane arrangements for the works. A CTIA assesses how construction and heavy-vehicle traffic affect the wider network, usually to satisfy a planning permit condition.

How long does approval take?

We can turn the design around in a few business days once we have the scope. Council permits and DTP MoAs then commonly take one to several weeks, so engage us early.
