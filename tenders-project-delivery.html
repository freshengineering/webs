Tender Support & Project Delivery

Winning the right work and delivering it cleanly both come down to the traffic methodology you put forward at tender. Fresh Engineering provides tender support and project delivery services that put a credible, costed and compliant traffic approach into your submission, then carry that approach through design, authorisations and on-site delivery once the contract is awarded. The result is a bid that prices the real scope rather than a guess, and a project that does not unravel when staging, road occupancy charges or authority conditions hit during construction.

In Victoria, traffic management is rarely a minor line item. On DTP and major project work it drives program, road occupancy costs, third-party approvals and stakeholder exposure, so getting the methodology wrong at tender can erode margin for the life of the job. We bring 15+ years of experience across small council projects through to North East Link, the West Gate Tunnel, the Metro Tunnel, the Level Crossing Removal Program and the Barwon Heads Road upgrade, which means we know how head contractors, DTP and councils actually assess and administer this work.

We work as an extension of your estimating and delivery teams. At tender we build the staging logic, identify the constraints and feed realistic quantities and durations into your pricing. After award we produce the Traffic Management Designs, road safety audits and supporting documentation needed to secure authorisation from the relevant authority and keep the works moving.

How tender support and delivery works

Our involvement is structured around the two phases that matter most: getting the bid right, then delivering against it. During tender we interrogate the scope, develop a staging strategy and quantify the traffic effort so your submission reflects buildability rather than optimism. Through delivery we convert that strategy into authorised designs and the evidence the principal and authority require.

Engaging us early in the tender period gives the most value, because the staging methodology often influences plant, program and even the construction sequence your estimators assume. Late involvement still helps, but the cheapest savings are found before the numbers are locked in.

  • Tender phase: review of scope, constraints, road network and authority requirements
  • Development of staging methodology and traffic management concept to support the bid narrative
  • Realistic quantities, durations and assumptions to inform pricing and road occupancy costs
  • Post-award: detailed Traffic Management Designs (TMDs) and staging plans
  • Road safety audits at the relevant project stage and design reviews
  • Ongoing support through construction including design revisions and authority liaison

Standards and authorities we work to

All designs are prepared in line with the Austroads Guide to Temporary Traffic Management (AGTTM) and the relevant DTP supplements, codes of practice and worksite requirements. Where the works affect the declared arterial road network, designs and occupancies are coordinated with DTP through the Road Occupancy and Memorandum of Authorisation processes; on local roads we deal directly with the responsible council and its conditions of consent.

We hold Traffic Management Designer (TMD) accreditation across Categories 1, 2 and 3, are an Accredited Road Safety Auditor and hold road safety barrier accreditation, so the documentation we issue carries the credentials clients and principal contractors expect on Victorian projects.

  • Local council permit and conditions of consent requirements
  • TMD Category 1, 2 and 3 design accreditation

Frequently Asked Questions

When do I need traffic methodology input at tender stage?

Any time the works occupy or affect a trafficked road, the methodology drives program, occupancy costs and authority approvals, so it should be considered the moment you start pricing. On DTP arterial and major project work it is effectively unavoidable, because road occupancy assumptions and the Memorandum of Authorisation process directly shape the bid. Engaging a designer before the numbers are locked in is the most reliable way to avoid pricing a methodology you cannot actually build.

How long does a Traffic Management Design take to produce after award?

A straightforward single-stage council TMD can be turned around in a few days to a week, while complex multi-stage major project designs take longer and often run in parallel with construction as staging is refined. Timeframes depend on authority review periods, the number of stages and how quickly site information and survey are available. We recommend confirming the authority's authorisation lead times early, as these frequently sit on the program's critical path.

What affects the cost of tender support and project delivery?

The main drivers are the road environment (local road versus DTP declared arterial), the number of construction stages, the complexity of occupancies and the level of authority liaison and road safety auditing required. A single-stage local job is modest; a multi-stage arterial or major project package with barrier design and audits involves considerably more effort. We scope and quote against your actual project rather than applying a flat rate.

Do I need an accredited Traffic Management Designer for my project?

Yes. In Victoria, temporary traffic management designs on the road network must be prepared by an accredited TMD at the appropriate category for the road type and complexity, in line with the AGTTM and DTP requirements. Fresh Engineering holds TMD Category 1, 2 and 3 accreditation, so we can prepare and certify designs from local roads through to high-volume arterials and major projects.
