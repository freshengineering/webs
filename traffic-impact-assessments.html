Traffic Impact Assessments for Planning

A Traffic Impact Assessment (TIA) is a technical report that quantifies how a proposed development will affect the surrounding road network, intersections, parking supply and access arrangements. In Victoria, councils and the Department of Transport and Planning (DTP) routinely require a TIA to support a planning permit application, particularly where a development generates significant additional vehicle movements or alters access to a road in a Road Zone. The report gives the responsible authority the evidence it needs to be satisfied that traffic, parking and access can be accommodated safely and efficiently.

Whether you are lodging a permit for a service station, a multi-unit residential development, a childcare centre, an industrial estate or a major mixed-use precinct, a well-prepared TIA can be the difference between a smooth assessment and a request for further information that stalls your timeline for months. Fresh Engineering prepares clear, defensible assessments that anticipate the questions a council traffic engineer or DTP referral officer will ask, and that stand up to scrutiny at VCAT if a matter proceeds to a hearing.

We bring 15+ years of Melbourne and regional Victorian experience to every assessment, from small council applications through to access strategies on major projects such as North East Link and the Level Crossing Removal Project. Our reports are grounded in current Austroads guidance, the Victoria Planning Provisions and DTP requirements, and written to be genuinely useful to the decision-maker.

Our TIA Process

A robust TIA follows a logical sequence, building from existing conditions through to the assessed impact and any treatments required. We tailor the depth of each stage to the scale of the proposal and the expectations of the relevant authority, so a small infill development is not burdened with the analysis appropriate to a regional shopping centre.

Early engagement matters. Where access to a Road Zone Category 1 is involved (triggering DTP as a referral authority), or where the trip generation is likely to be material, we recommend a pre-application discussion with council or DTP to confirm scope, the design horizon and the intersections to be modelled before the analysis begins.

  • Review the proposal, planning context and relevant permit triggers, including any Road Zone access requirements under Clause 52.29
  • Establish existing conditions through traffic surveys, site inspection and a review of crash history
  • Estimate traffic generation using the RTA Guide to Traffic Generating Developments and Austroads trip rates appropriate to the land use
  • Distribute and assign trips to the surrounding network and key intersections
  • Undertake intersection analysis (SIDRA Intersection) for the relevant scenarios and design horizon
  • Assess access design, sight distance, swept paths and on-site circulation against Austroads and AS 2890
  • Identify any required treatments and document findings in a clear, permit-ready report

Standards and Authorities

Our assessments are prepared in accordance with the Austroads Guide to Traffic Management and Guide to Road Design, and reflect current DTP and VicRoads practice for developments affecting the declared road network. We address the relevant Victoria Planning Provisions, including car parking under Clause 52.06, access to a Road Zone Category 1 under Clause 52.29, and loading provisions, so the traffic and parking case is consistent across the application.

Where a proposal fronts or accesses a Road Zone Category 1, DTP is a referral authority under Clause 66 and its requirements shape the scope. We understand what DTP expects in terms of intersection performance, access spacing and safety, and we structure reports to satisfy both the council and the state authority in a single document.

  • Austroads Guide to Traffic Management and Guide to Road Design
  • DTP / VicRoads access management and intersection performance requirements
  • Clause 52.06 car parking and Clause 52.29 access to a Road Zone Category 1
  • AS 2890 (parking and access) and AS/NZS 2890.6 (accessible parking)
  • SIDRA Intersection for capacity and performance analysis
  • Local council engineering and infrastructure design standards

Frequently Asked Questions

When is a Traffic Impact Assessment required for a planning permit in Victoria?

A TIA is generally required when a development is expected to generate significant additional vehicle traffic, alters access to the road network, or accesses a Road Zone Category 1 where DTP is a referral authority under Clause 52.29. Councils also commonly request one for higher-intensity uses such as childcare centres, service stations, supermarkets and larger residential developments. The simplest way to confirm is a pre-application discussion with the council, or we can advise based on your specific proposal and site.

How long does a Traffic Impact Assessment take to prepare?

A straightforward assessment typically takes around two to three weeks once we have the development plans and any required traffic surveys. Timing depends on whether new survey counts are needed (these must avoid school holidays and atypical conditions, so they can add lead time) and on the number of intersections to be modelled. We give you a clear timeframe at the outset so it fits your lodgement program.

What affects the cost of a TIA?

The main cost drivers are the scale and complexity of the development, the number of intersections requiring SIDRA modelling, whether new traffic surveys are needed, and whether DTP is involved as a referral authority. A single-access infill development is far less involved than a multi-access regional retail proposal. We provide a fixed-fee proposal after reviewing your plans and site so there are no surprises.

Will the TIA hold up if my application goes to VCAT?

Yes. We prepare every assessment to be defensible, using recognised Austroads methodology, established trip rates and transparent assumptions, so the analysis stands up to scrutiny. If a matter proceeds to VCAT, we can provide expert evidence and respond to issues raised by council, DTP or objectors.
