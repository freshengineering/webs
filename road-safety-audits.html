Independent Road Safety Audits

A road safety audit is an independent check on how a road, intersection or worksite could lead to someone being killed or seriously injured. It's not a compliance tick against the drawings. It asks where the crash risk sits, even on a design that meets every standard.

We run accredited audits across Melbourne metro and regional Victoria, from concept through to pre-opening, plus existing roads and work zones. With TMD Cat 1/2/3 credentials and 15+ years on jobs like North East Link, the West Gate Tunnel and the Level Crossing Removal Program, we know how a road actually gets built.

Audit stages we cover

We follow the Austroads Guide to Road Safety Part 6, which sets out the stages so risk gets caught when it's cheapest to fix. We match the depth of review to the maturity of the design and pick up existing roads and work-zone traffic management too.

  • Feasibility - route and layout options before commitment
  • Preliminary design - geometry, sight distance, provision for pedestrians and cyclists
  • Detailed design - signs, marking, barriers, lighting
  • Pre-opening - site inspection, including a night visit
  • Existing roads and active work zones

Independent, and to DTP standard

The auditor sits outside the design team. That independence is what DTP, councils and Tier 1 contractors expect on the record, and it's what makes the findings stick when an authority reviews them.

  • Audit team separate from the designers
  • Assessed against Austroads Part 6 and DTP supplements
  • Meets DTP and council audit requirements
  • Carries weight with the approving authority

What you get back

A report a designer can act on, not a list of vague observations. Each finding is located, the road users at risk are named, and the risk is ranked so you know what to fix first.

  • Risk-ranked findings by location
  • Practical, buildable recommendations
  • Annotated plans and site photos
  • Response table to close out each finding
  • Optional close-out meeting

Frequently Asked Questions

When is a road safety audit required in Victoria?

Audits are routine on DTP works and often come in as a planning permit condition for traffic-generating developments. They're also expected on Big Build packages like the Level Crossing Removal Program.

Who actually does the audit?

An accredited road safety auditor working independently of the design team. That separation is what DTP and councils require for the audit to count.

How is this different from a compliance check?

A compliance check confirms the design meets the standards. An audit asks how the road could still contribute to a serious crash, even when it's fully compliant.
