Car Parking Demand Assessments

A car parking demand assessment works out how much parking a use actually generates, then sets that against what Clause 52.06 of the planning scheme requires. Where the two don't match, the report gives the responsible authority the evidence to grant a permit with fewer spaces.

Council and DTP want to know a reduced provision won't spill onto nearby streets or create a safety problem. We answer that with real demand figures, not just the statutory rate, so the report holds up at the council desk and at VCAT.

How we assess demand

We confirm the Clause 52.06 statutory rate for each use, including any parking overlay or council precinct plan rate that applies. Then we measure likely demand from empirical surveys of comparable sites and the actual operating pattern of the use.

  • Statutory rate under Clause 52.06, plus any overlay or precinct rate
  • Empirical demand surveys at comparable sites
  • Public transport, walking and cycling access
  • Shared-parking opportunities across uses
  • Provision tested against the statutory requirement

Justifying a reduction

A parking dispensation under Clause 52.06-3 lets the authority cut the requirement, even to nil, where it's justified against the decision guidelines. We quantify the real demand and show the reduced provision will operate without unreasonable impact on surrounding streets.

  • Reduction quantified against the statutory figure
  • Case built on the Clause 52.06-3 decision guidelines
  • On-street parking conditions reviewed
  • Layout checked against AS 2890.1, 2890.2 and 2890.6
  • Expert evidence at VCAT where it's needed

What you receive

A permit-ready demand and supply report, written the way an assessing planner reads it. We flag any layout problems and can mark up the architectural plans before lodgement to head off a request for further information.

  • Standalone demand and supply report
  • Statutory requirement, assessed demand and reduction case
  • Survey data and comparable-site evidence
  • Bay, aisle, ramp and accessway compliance check

Frequently Asked Questions

When do I need a car parking demand assessment?

When a proposal can't meet the Clause 52.06 rate and you're seeking a reduction, or when council asks for one to support a new, intensified or change-of-use application. Uses like childcare, medical and hospitality often need one because the statutory rate fits them poorly.

How does a parking reduction under Clause 52.06 work?

Clause 52.06-3 lets the responsible authority reduce the requirement, even to zero, if the reduction is justified against the decision guidelines. We back that with empirical demand data and the site's transport access.

How long does an assessment take?

Usually one to two weeks once plans and the brief are confirmed. It runs longer where empirical surveys are needed, since those have to be done on representative days and avoid school and public holidays.
