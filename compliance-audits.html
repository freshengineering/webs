Traffic Management Compliance Audits

A Traffic Management Compliance Audit is an independent, on-site verification that a deployed traffic management setup actually matches the approved Traffic Guidance Scheme (TGS), the relevant permit conditions, and the requirements of the Austroads Guide to Temporary Traffic Management (AGTTM). It is distinct from designing the scheme or holding the permit — it is a separate set of expert eyes confirming that what is on the ground reflects what was approved on paper, and that road users and workers are being protected as intended.

In Victoria, the move to AGTTM under the Department of Transport and Planning (DTP) framework has raised the bar on accountability. A scheme that looks compliant on a drawing can fail in the field because of an inadequate taper length, an incorrectly placed advance warning sign, a sight-distance obstruction, or a temporary speed limit that was never properly authorised. On DTP-managed and council roads — and on Tier 1 projects with strict client and principal-contractor requirements — those gaps carry real safety, legal and reputational consequences.

How a compliance audit works

A compliance audit follows a structured, repeatable process so the findings are objective and defensible. We start with a desktop review of the approved documents — the TGS, the Memorandum of Authorisation for any temporary speed limit, road occupation licences or permits, and any conditions imposed by DTP, the council or the asset owner. We then attend site, ideally unannounced or at a representative time, and assess the setup against those documents and against AGTTM in real operating conditions.

On site we measure and photograph the controls that matter: advance warning sequences, sign spacing and legibility, taper and buffer lengths, lateral clearances, delineation, positive protection where required, sight distance, and the management of pedestrians, cyclists and other vulnerable road users. Each observation is recorded against a specific clause or approved-plan reference so the report stands up to scrutiny.

  • Desktop review of the approved TGS, permit and any temporary speed limit authorisation
  • On-site inspection under live traffic conditions, with measurements and dated photos
  • Assessment against AGTTM, the approved scheme and permit conditions
  • Verification of signage, tapers, buffers, delineation and positive protection
  • Check of pedestrian, cyclist and property-access provisions
  • Same-day notification of any critical (life-safety) non-conformances

Standards and authorities we audit against

Audits are benchmarked against the documents that actually govern the work in Victoria. The primary technical reference is the Austroads Guide to Temporary Traffic Management (AGTTM), supported by relevant Austroads guides and DTP supplements and standard drawings. Where works occur on the declared road network, DTP (formerly VicRoads) requirements and the conditions attached to the road occupation licence apply; on local roads, the responsible council's requirements and any consent conditions take precedence.

We also confirm that the people and authorisations underpinning the setup are valid — that the scheme was prepared by a suitably accredited designer, that any temporary speed limit is properly authorised under a Memorandum of Authorisation rather than self-imposed, and that the implementation matches the category and complexity the scheme was approved for.

  • Austroads Guide to Temporary Traffic Management (AGTTM) and DTP supplements
  • DTP / VicRoads road occupation licence and conditions of approval
  • Local council consent conditions on municipal roads
  • Temporary speed limits authorised under a Memorandum of Authorisation
  • Designer accreditation and scheme category appropriate to the works

What you receive

The deliverable is a clear, formatted compliance audit report you can hand to a client, principal contractor or the road authority. It states what was inspected, when, and under what conditions, then lists each finding ranked by risk — from critical non-conformances requiring immediate rectification through to minor and observational items. Every non-conformance references the relevant AGTTM clause or approved-plan detail and includes a practical recommendation to close it out.

Where a critical safety issue is identified, we notify you on site so it can be corrected immediately rather than waiting for the written report. Re-inspection to confirm close-out can be included where required.

  • Written compliance audit report with site details, date and conditions
  • Findings ranked critical / major / minor / observation
  • Each non-conformance tied to a specific clause or plan reference
  • Dated photographic evidence and measurements
  • Practical, prioritised rectification recommendations
  • Optional re-inspection and sign-off once issues are closed out

Who it's for and typical projects

Compliance audits suit any party that needs independent assurance that a traffic management setup is doing what it should. Tier 1 and civil contractors use them to satisfy client and principal-contractor obligations and to demonstrate due diligence. Councils and asset owners commission them to verify works occurring under permit on their network, and developers use them on works affecting public roads during construction.

Typical engagements range from a single high-risk lane closure or set of intersection works through to ongoing audit programs across major infrastructure packages.

  • Tier 1 and civil contractors demonstrating due diligence and client compliance
  • Local councils and asset owners verifying permitted works on their roads
  • Developers undertaking works affecting the public road network
  • Major project packages such as road upgrades, level crossing and tunnel works
  • High-risk activities: lane and road closures, detours, night works, intersection works

Frequently Asked Questions

When is an independent traffic management compliance audit required in Victoria?

There is no single rule mandating one for every job, but they are increasingly required by permit conditions, principal-contractor procedures and client specifications, particularly on DTP-managed roads and major projects. They are most valuable on higher-risk activities — road and lane closures, detours, night works and complex intersections — and after incidents or near-misses where you need an independent record of conformance.

What is the difference between a traffic management compliance audit and a road safety audit?

A road safety audit is a proactive review of a road or design to identify crash risk, usually at a design or pre-opening stage in accordance with the Austroads road safety audit process. A compliance audit verifies that an installed temporary traffic management setup matches its approved TGS, permit conditions and AGTTM. They answer different questions — one asks 'is this safe?', the other asks 'does this match what was approved?' — and Fresh Engineering provides both.

How long does a compliance audit take and how quickly do we get the report?

A typical single-site audit is around a half-day on site depending on the size and complexity of the setup, with the written report generally issued within a few business days. Any critical, life-safety non-conformances are flagged to you on site immediately so they can be rectified straight away rather than waiting for the report.

What factors affect the cost of a compliance audit?

Cost is driven by site complexity and size, the number and spread of setups to be audited, travel to regional Victoria, whether night or out-of-hours attendance is needed, and whether re-inspection and close-out sign-off are required.
